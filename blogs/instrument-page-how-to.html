Your Instrument Page: Charts, News, Technicals, Fundamentals

Master the instrument page on finstrument.ai—deep-link to any symbol, customize charts, read auto technicals, and scan news & fundamentals in one place.

Open any symbol instantly

Go to the Instrument page with a symbol query string: ?tvwidgetsymbol=EXCHANGE:SYMBOL. Examples: NSE:TCS, NASDAQ:AAPL, BITSTAMP:BTCUSD, OANDA:USDINR. This deep link loads the advanced chart and all related widgets for that instrument.

Advanced chart basics

The chart supports multiple timeframes (1m to 1M), indicators, and drawings. Popular starting points:

  • Trend view: 1D/1W with 20/50/200 DMAs and volume.
  • Momentum: RSI(14) and MACD for strength/turns.
  • Levels: Prior swing highs/lows, gap zones, anchored VWAPs.
Example indicator stack
  • MAs: 20/50/200
  • RSI(14) for momentum regime
  • Volume + optional OBV/CMF

Auto Technical Analysis

The Technical Analysis widget provides an automated buy/neutral/sell summary across timeframes. Use it as a quick companion to the chart—not a replacement. Align the signal with your timeframe (e.g., 1D/1W for swing, 1H/4H for short-term).

Top Stories & context

The Top Stories widget aggregates instrument-related headlines. After a big move on the chart, check whether news confirms the catalyst or if it’s purely technical. For macro sensitivity, cross-check the Economic Calendar.

Company Profile & fundamentals

For equities, the Company Profile widget shows sector, market cap, ratios, and other basics. Non-equity instruments (FX pairs, indices, crypto, commodities) may show limited or no fundamentals—use the chart/technicals instead.

Theme & readability

finstrument.ai syncs with your system theme. If you change dark/light while the page is open, refresh to reload widgets in the new theme, ensuring consistent charts and panels across the page.

Workflow tips

  • From screen to chart: Start on Screeners, then click a candidate to open its instrument page.
  • Cross-asset context: Check Markets and Heatmaps before making decisions.
  • Timeframe alignment: Confirm setups on one higher timeframe than you trade.
  • Risk control: Size positions with ATR% or recent swing levels; avoid trading into major events (see Calendar).
